REQUEST FOR PROPOSAL (RFP)
Project: Unified Citizen Service Portal Modernization
Client: City of Redwood Heights
RFP ID: RH-2026-DIGI-014
Issue Date: April 15, 2026
Proposal Due Date: May 20, 2026

1. BUSINESS CONTEXT
The City of Redwood Heights is seeking a delivery partner to modernize citizen-facing services currently spread across legacy web forms and manual workflows. The objective is to launch a unified digital portal that improves resident experience, reduces turnaround time for permits and requests, and provides leadership-level visibility on service KPIs.

2. PROJECT GOALS
- Create a single portal for permit requests, utility service requests, complaint submissions, and appointment scheduling.
- Reduce average case turnaround time by 30% in year one.
- Improve first-contact resolution with integrated knowledge workflows.
- Meet WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility requirements.

3. SCOPE OF WORK
The selected vendor will provide end-to-end services including discovery, solution architecture, implementation, testing, deployment, training, and hypercare support.

Expected delivery is structured in four phases:
- Phase 1 (4 weeks): Discovery, process mapping, architecture blueprint.
- Phase 2 (8 weeks): Core platform build and integrations.
- Phase 3 (10 weeks): User acceptance testing, security hardening, and data migration.
- Phase 4 (4 weeks): Go-live, onboarding, and stabilization.

Target completion timeline: 6 months from contract signing.
Note: City leadership has requested an accelerated 4-month launch if possible.

4. TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
Required technologies and capabilities:
- Frontend: React, responsive design, multilingual support.
- Backend APIs: FastAPI or equivalent service framework.
- Data layer: PostgreSQL with auditable change history.
- Infrastructure: Azure Kubernetes Service (AKS) with CI/CD.
- Identity: Azure AD SSO and role-based access control.
- Observability: centralized logging, metrics dashboards, and alerting.
- Analytics: Power BI integration for executive reporting.

The vendor must expose secure APIs for integration with:
- Existing ERP billing system.
- GIS mapping service for location-based requests.
- State-level identity verification service.

5. DELIVERABLES
- Discovery and solution design document.
- Architecture decision record (ADR) package.
- Working citizen portal with role-based workflows.
- API integration layer and interface contracts.
- Automated test suite and release pipeline.
- Security and compliance assessment report.
- Training materials and administrator playbook.

6. STAKEHOLDERS
Key stakeholders include:
- Priya Raman, Chief Information Officer (Executive Sponsor)
- Daniel Ortiz, Director of Citizen Services (Business Owner)
- Aisha Khan, PMO Lead (Program Governance)
- Leo Murphy, Security and Compliance Lead
- Procurement Steering Committee

7. DEPENDENCIES AND ASSUMPTIONS
- Access to ERP integration documentation by project week 2.
- Availability of GIS sandbox credentials before sprint 3.
- State identity API contract finalized before Phase 2 completion.
- Legal approval for data retention policy updates.

8. ACCEPTANCE CRITERIA
The selected partner must define measurable acceptance criteria for each module and workflow. Current RFP sections list expected outcomes but do not yet include threshold-based test metrics for every citizen journey.

9. RISK CONSIDERATIONS
Potential concerns identified by the City:
- Compressed schedule may conflict with mandatory security testing windows.
- Third-party state identity API currently has unstable response times.
- Legacy ERP integration behavior is partially undocumented.
- Budget penalties apply if SLA targets are missed for two consecutive months post go-live.
- Non-compliance with privacy obligations may trigger legal and contractual action.

10. PROPOSAL INSTRUCTIONS
Vendors must include:
- Team structure, named roles, and delivery governance model.
- Detailed timeline with critical path assumptions.
- Risk mitigation approach and contingency planning.
- Cost breakdown by phase and support period.
- At least two comparable government or public-sector implementation references.
